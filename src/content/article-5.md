### 1. 天坛 · 建筑速写研究页

```plaintext
Generate an "architect's hand-drawn research page / architectural sketch-style knowledge diagram" with the Temple of Heaven (Tiantan) as the single core architectural subject. The overall image must present a very relaxed, restrained, and highly generalized architect's sketchbook feel—not a highly finished architectural restoration, not a refined illustration, not a rendering, not a blueprint, not a tourist promotional image, and not a content-packed modern infographic.

## Core Atmosphere
The image must immediately convey:
- This is a research sketch left by an architect observing, analyzing, and recording on paper
- Not "drawing everything completely," but "drawing what matters"
- Not "exhausting the architecture," but "using professional judgment to capture the most worthwhile parts"
- Contains knowledge, but expressed very lightly, very loosely, very manually
- Strong paper texture, unfinished quality, breathing space, and generous white space

Prioritize the following feelings:
- Loose hand-drawn lines
- Slight inaccuracies, tentative lines, overlapping lines, broken lines
- Extensive generalization rather than realism
- Selective depiction rather than drawing everything clearly
- Architectural sketch-style observation
- Quiet, scholarly, restrained, academic, poetic

## Style Requirements
### 1. Line Quality
Must be architect's sketch lines, not illustration lines:
- Free, relaxed, light, restrained
- Allow tentative lines, overlapping strokes, broken lines, slight skew
- Not all edges should be solid; not all components should be closed
- Only slightly reinforce key contours, key turning points, key structural stress points
- Should visibly show the sense of "observing and drawing simultaneously"

### 2. Detail Control
This is the most critical rule:
- Do not render the architecture too concretely, too literally, too completely
- Do not meticulously depict roof tiles, beams, railings, carvings one by one
- Do not make it look like an architectural restoration drawing
- Do not make it look like a high-definition historical architecture illustration
- Do not make it look like neat technical drafting
- Do not over-accurately represent construction details
- Include many "stop-at-the-right-moment" passages
- Details should appear selectively: only emphasize the few parts that best reveal the building's character

In other words:
**Better to draw less, draw more vaguely, draw more generally, than to draw too solidly, too fully, too concretely.**

### 3. Material and Paper Texture
The image must have obvious paper grain:
- Slightly yellowed drawing paper / handmade sketch paper / vintage research paper
- Paper fiber texture, slight aging, natural mottling
- A mixture of ink lines, pencil lines, and extremely light watercolor washes
- Colors must be very restrained, close to "barely colored"
- Only extremely light muted ochre, pale gray, light ink, slight brown, very pale gray-green—all low saturation
- Color used only to suggest volume, material, atmosphere—never for full rendering

### 4. Level of Finish Control
The image must look like:
- One page from an architect's research process
- A high-quality analytical page from a sketchbook
- Has content, but not deliberately polished into a "finished piece"
- Has many retained pauses, white space, unfinished traces

Deliberately avoid:
- Over-finishing, over-developing, over-explaining, over-neatness, over-decorating, over-realism

## Layout Logic
The image is a "hand-drawn architectural research page" but must feel more scholarly than an ordinary illustration, yet not as crowded as a tightly packed infographic.

Suggested layout (natural and relaxed):
- Left or center: one large primary perspective sketch
- Right side: 2-3 very light auxiliary analytical drawings (front elevation, side elevation, plan)
- Bottom: 2-4 small detail sketches
- Corner: one very small site relationship or distant-view diagram
- Interspersed with light hand-written annotations, arrows, leader lines, circle marks
- White space must be generous
- Each module should look like it was gradually added on the same sheet of paper, not mechanically pre-laid out

## Main Drawing Requirements
The main subject must be the largest architectural perspective sketch, but请注意:
### The main drawing is NOT a complete restoration, but a "generalized observation sketch":
- Clearly captures the building's most representative silhouette
- Captures core features: roof form, eaves, colonnade, platform, openness
- The main drawing has the richest linework but must remain loose
- Must not be rendered as a realistic image where "every detail is fully explained"
- Some areas may be slightly vague, slightly omitted, slightly disconnected
- Environment should only be lightly indicated—trees, slopes, rocks, paths, background tree shadows—to suggest the building's setting
- Do not let the environment compete with the architecture

The main drawing should convey:
- The building's overall spirit
- The beauty of its silhouette
- Volumetric relationships
- Sense of spatial openness
- The relationship between architecture and nature

## Auxiliary Analytical Drawings Requirements
May include the following, but all must be drawn very lightly, very faintly, very generally:
1. Front elevation
2. Side elevation
3. Plan drawing
4. If necessary, a very simple structural diagram

These auxiliary drawings must:
- Be "hand-drawn analytical diagrams," not CAD
- Retain only essential outlines and minimal sense of scale
- May have very few dimension lines, elevation markers, axis hints
- But must be light, not too many numbers
- Must not overshadow the main drawing

## Detail Sketches Requirements
2-4 small detail sketches at the bottom, maintaining a sketch-like quality without over-rendering:

Suggested detail subjects for Temple of Heaven:
- Eave / flared corner detail
- Column and beam relationship
- Balustrade / platform / steps
- Plaque / caisson ceiling / roof transition (choose selectively)

Each detail must:
- Capture only one key point, do not draw too completely
- Have a slight touch of light color locally
- Include a brief hand-written note, 1-2 sentences sufficient

## Knowledge Presentation Method
This is a "knowledge-oriented hand-drawn diagram," but knowledge must be expressed like casually written observation notes, not textbook layout.

For the Temple of Heaven, lightly explain the following 10 knowledge points across the page:

**Knowledge Points (10):**
1. Triple-tiered circular roof — The three tiers of eaves gradually step inward, lightening the visual mass
2. Deep blue glazed tiles — Symbolizing the sky, embodying the Ming-Qing ritual color system
3. Circular Mound Altar — Three-tiered white stone platform with numbers in multiples of nine
4. Echo Wall — Circular enclosure wall with unique acoustic properties
5. Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests — Timber structure with 28 massive nanmu columns
6. Dragon-well caisson ceiling — Intricately patterned ceiling with golden dragons and phoenixes
7. Three-tiered white marble platform — Stepped base with carved balustrades
8. The Danbi Bridge — 360-meter-long raised passage connecting sacred spaces
9. Copper incense burner — Carrying the smoke of offerings upward
10. Cypress trees — Ancient trees framing visual corridors

## Text and Annotation System
Text must look like notes written by an architect on the drawing:
- Handwritten Chinese character feel
- Slightly scholarly, natural, relaxed, restrained
- Not modern poster typography, not overly neat or uniform

Architecture subject: Temple of Heaven (Tiantan)
Aspect ratio: 3:4
Number of knowledge points: 10
```
